VOE Fund Description

VOE tracks the CRSP US Mid Cap Value Index which classifies stocks based on five value factors.

VOE Factset Analytics Insight

VOE captures the broad US midcap value market. It doesn't stray into small-caps as many peers do, although it does emphasize different sectors than its MSCI benchmark. The underlying index is constructed using a multi-factor model that classifies value securities in the midcap market based on book to price, forward earnings to price, historic earnings to price, dividend-to-price ratio and sales-to-price ratio. Securities are scored and ranked based on the composite of these five factors. As with all Vanguard ETFs, transparency is a slight concern - holdings are only disclosed monthly, with a lag.

VOE MSCI ESG Analytics Insight

Vanguard Mid-Cap Value ETF has an MSCI ESG Fund Rating of A based on a score of 6.12 out of 10. The MSCI ESG Fund Rating measures the resiliency of portfolios to long-term risks and opportunities arising from environmental, social, and governance factors. ESG Fund Ratings range from best (AAA) to worst (CCC). Highly rated funds consist of companies that tend to show strong and/or improving management of financially relevant environmental, social and governance issues. These companies may be more resilient to disruptions arising from ESG events.

The funds Peer Rank reflects the ranking of a funds MSCI ESG Fund Quality Score against the scores of other funds within the same peer group, as defined by the Thomson Reuters Lipper Global Classification. Vanguard Mid-Cap Value ETF ranks in the 59th percentile within its peer group and in the 95th percentile within the global universe of all funds covered by MSCI ESG Fund Ratings.
